Why I chose Biomedical Engineering:

My journey to Biomedical Engineering (BME) began in high school, where I delved into AP Biology, AP Calculus, and AP Physics. During this period, my fascination with biology was sparked by specific topics, notably protein synthesis. As someone who frequented the gym, understanding the intricacies of protein became more than just academic; it became a practical and personal interest.

Calculus quickly emerged as a favorite, providing me with a deep appreciation for its logical connections and cohesive nature. Physics, especially in the context of kinematics, allowed me to bridge concepts to mathematics, creating a comprehensive understanding of STEM subjects.

Although I enjoyed biology, math, and physics individually, the prospect of limiting myself to just one discipline didn't resonate with me. After thoughtful consideration and guidance from my AP biology teacher, I discovered the interdisciplinary world of biomedical engineering.

This major not only accommodated my diverse interests but also aligned with my aspirations to contribute to the medical field. The realization solidified during my volunteering stint at Centrastate Medical Center in Freehold, where I witnessed the impact of technology on healthcare.

Now, as I reflect on my journey, it's evident that biomedical engineering is not just a major but a path I'm committed to exploring and dedicating my career to. The amalgamation of biology, mathematics, physics, and a direct impact on healthcare resonates deeply with my professional aspirations.
